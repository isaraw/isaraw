  • More Than Once a Night Men

    Public awareness and HCPs pharma campaign

About

The Company is a science-led global healthcare company that researches and develops a broad range of innovative products in three primary areas of Pharmaceuticals, Vaccines and Consumer Healthcare.

The aim was to launch a public health awareness campaign ‘More Than Once a Night’ to promote awareness of a disease called Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) among Irish senior males ages over 50. This was following healthcare professionals’ feedback suggesting that there was a low level of awareness of the disease among their patients. Symptoms of BPH can affect men’s quality of life, day to day activities and cause embarrassment, yet many Irish men feel embarrassed to visit their GP.   

The Challenge

Target Audience for this campaign included Irish Senior males, HCPs and GPs. 

There were a number of challenges, including:

  • Making information available in different forms; Website, Video, Downloadable information, Questionnaires. 
  • Compliance - Medical & Technical  
  • Legal and Medical constraints 
  • The company had never advertised on Social Media 
  • Delicate issue 
  • Reaching a very specific audience on many channels 
  • Cutting through the noise of advertising to GPs --- they have little time available  

Strategy and Implementation  

Target Audience for this campaign included Irish Senior males, HCPs and GPs. 

Combined Media and the company created a fully integrated, multi-channel digital campaign to to draw in the appropriate demographics of users.
We researched older males presence on a number of channels including Social Media.
In Ireland, it has been estimated that 85% of households have access to the internet at home in 2015. 29% of the 60-74 age category use it everyday. source.
Digital marketing activities included: Google Ads (Search and Display), Banner Advertising, Facebook ads, Search engine optimisation, Email Banner Marketing, Remarketing and a third party integrated PR campaign featuring Brendan Grace as an ambassador / advocate.

Website


A bespoke website was created for the More Than Once a Night Campaign featuring the comedian Brendan Grace, who partnered with The Ireland to launch the public health awareness campaign. Due to the target audience we made sure that we were creating a "senior friendly" website.
We devised a simple navigation system and the landing pages were specifically designed to appeal to a senior audience. Age appropriate assets were created.
Useful information and practical content was created and implemented on the website including; a questionnaire, video and information about BPH available to download.
Goals were set up and tracking was put in place for different categories so that they everything could be measured including video views, PDF downloads, and questionnaires that were completed.
Search Engine Optimisation (SEO) was implemented on the site. We worked to improve website rankings on search engines through offsite and onsite SEO including onpage technical SEO. Constant monitoring of organic performance was reported.

Video
People will watch more than they read, so a bigger part of the message was to resonate through the video.The video walks you through the symptoms and effects BPH has. We knew that the target audience might have slower internet connection so videos were optimised.

Questionnaire
A written screening tool (questionnaire) was created and available to fill out. After filling out the questionnaire your International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS) is calculated giving you a reading. As it is a delicate issue to talk about and people can be embarrassed, the information is then available to print and give to your doctor on your next visit.

Results

All relevant metrics were tracked in Google Analytics, SEO Moz and channel-specific tracking platforms, including videos view and completions, users experience and behaviour on the website, search engine performance, social media activity, trends, conversions and others. 
The campaign was very successuful and for this reason the client decided to run an extension campaign after the first phase ended.
The cammpaign's results were compared with industry benchmarks and benchmarks for the various channels, showing that the campaign outperformed most benchmarks provided by the medical press in terms of emails open rates and click-through-rates (GPBuddy, Medical Independent, Irish Medical Directory); in the public awareness campaign:

  • Google Search overall CTR was higher than 4% - with 2% considered average by Google;
  • Google Display Network CTR was higher than 0.2% - while Avg. Display Network CTR for Health Conditions (worldwide) is 0.05;
  • On Facebook, avg. CTR was higher than 4%; according to Salesforce benchmark report, Avg. Click Through Rate for Healthcare ads on Facebook is 0.433%;

On the website there were hundreds of PDF Downloads, Quiz Completed and Video views. Each source of traffic was evaluated and reanked in terms of converison rates and overall Performance. The campaign itself became a benchmark for all the following campaigns run for the same team or other teams for the same client.
